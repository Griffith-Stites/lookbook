Duality




 

Sun s warm washcloth on my face,

Wind s hands gently caress my hair,

Dress brushes across my smooth legs.

 

Necklace grabs at my thick neck,

slipping over and under my adam s apple.

Fabric sweats, strains its muscles

to fit over my shoulders.

 

Heeled boots push on my calves,

kindly adjust my posture.

Black nylon belt, which sports a bow tie,

lends me hips.

 

Hands of the boots cackle,

Pointing at my bulky calves.

Belt slips down my body,

blaming the lack of an expected guardrail.

 

Soft feel of my cheeks

Disgusting rigidity of my jaw

Smooth feel of my legs

Feeling of stubble on my face

 

Year to year

Month to month

Week to week

Day to day

Moment to moment

 

I may win battles

But the war
