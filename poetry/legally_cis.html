Legally Cis



3

April, 2019


Reach into the pocket

A little plastic card tells me


Go to the restroom

A little plastic sign tells me


Applying for a job

A rimmed bubble tells me


I constantly try to escape

My undesired destiny

My masculinity

My maleness


Day in and day out, I am reminded

Of a binary world

Of a masculine role

Of which I cannot escape



Commentary: This poem was written by the frustration of the society and government of the world I live in. I already have so many struggles internally with trying to tell myself that I am not just a man. Every time I feel like I make progress, I encounter a form or an encounter which reminds me of the reality I live in. That I am seen as a man, and the only options are man or woman.
