Preferred Pronouns

They are not my preferred pronouns, they are my pronouns. Often, I hear people ask me to state my preferred pronouns. I know their heart is in the right place, but I also think that it is important to make it clear that using the correct pronouns is not an option. Using someone's correct pronouns is not something you do when it is convenient, it is something you ALWAYS do.

Using the correct pronouns is a way to signify importance and respect. It helps to humanize trans people. After all, the only people who it is societally acceptable to misgender are trans people. Trans people deserve the same respect that everyone else receives.

I think that the phrasing "Preferred Pronouns" is said with good intent. It is a way of saying "these are my pronouns, but it is okay if you mess up. After all, we are only human". While I agree it is okay to mess up on pronouns, because it can be hard, I do not think the way to communicate pronouns is softly.

I think it is important that pronoun corrections are said very directly. Pronouns are very important to most transgender people and should be handled as such. When too much concern is put over saying something softly, it can lose the importance of the issue.

At the same time, I do want to be clear that I understand pronouns ARE difficult. But we need to make sure the focus is on making the transgender person feel comfortable and valid in their gender instead of coddling the feelings of others for getting pronouns wrong.
