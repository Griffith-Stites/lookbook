I don't Know

Skeptics, doubters, trans "supporters", parents, and more:

I don't know why I am transgender. I DO NOT KNOW.

I don't know why trans people exist. In some ways, I almost wish they did not. The experience of being in the wrong body is awful. It is something that cis people could never understand.

I constantly feel like all my bits and pieces are wrong. I get so envious of AFAB (assigned female at birth) people it hurts. I feel such regret for something I had no control over.

I don't know why trans people exist. But trans people DO exist and I am TRANS. I did not choose to be trans. But since I am trans, I am going to be my full self.

If you want to support me, spend more time actually SUPPORTING trans people instead of trying to understand them. You will never understand the trans experience. By very definition, you cannot.

So instead of trying to undermine and question my identity journey, cheer me on from the sidelines.

Mae
