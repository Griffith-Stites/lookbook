About Me

Non-binary Engineer & Diversity Advocate

Who I am

I am currently a student studying Electrical and Computer Engineering at Olin College of Engineering. I want to change the world both through engineering and through diversity advocacy work, especially in regards to LGBTQ issues and gender equality.

My focus so far has been on educating primary school students about the importance of diversity and understanding. My work in this area has been focused around telling my story as someone who is Non-binary AMAB.

Why I started this Site

I started this site out of frustration with men's fashion sites and guides on the internet. The emphasis on masculinity and gender norms made me feel uncomfortable and trapped - like I was being told to be something I was not. I want to help provide an example so others do not have to go through the same mental struggle.

Many of my friends have similar frustrations in relation to women's fashion and beauty expectations as they also do not conform to their gender norm. I want people to know that they can have fun with fashion and should feel free embrace both stereotypically masculine and feminine clothing. When I went off to college, I met a lot of other LGBT friends and they helped give me the confidence to explore fashion. Many of my friends feel uncomfortable in clothing that is not traditional for their gender.

I want to help breakdown gender norms and show others that they should feel free to be themselves without shame.

Originally this site was purely a fashion site, but I wanted the site to have more meaning. This led me to name this site "project they" as a way of expressing a mission of fighting gender norms and supporting gender non-conforming and transgender people everywhere.
