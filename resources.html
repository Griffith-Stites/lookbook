Resources

Basics

Transgender FAQ

Basics about the term "transgender", gender identity, pronouns, and allyship.

Trans Intro Playlist

Playlist of videos to give an introduction to trans lives, terminology, and issues.

Tips for Allies of Transgender People

Addresses common misconceptions and mistakes made by transgender allies. It addresses backhanded compliments, inappropriate questions, transitions, and how to make your spaces truly trans-inclusive.

LGBTQ+ Flag Books

Basic information about the various LGBTQ+ flags and identities.

LGBTQ+ Definitions

Trans Student Educational Resources list of LGBTQ+ definitions. It is a great lookup table for all sorts of transgender terminology.

PFLAG National Glossary of Terms

An extensive list of LGBTQ+ terminology.

My Pronouns

mypronouns.org has a number of resources on personal pronouns. This includes why they are important, how to use them, how to handle mistakes, and guides on sharing your own pronouns.



Parent Resources

4 Ways Parents Can Process Their Feelings After Their Kid Comes Out As Transgender

Explains the best way to deal with your feelings (as a parent) when your child comes out as trans. It is important to know how to deal with your feelings so they do not get directed to your child.

6 Signs That You Might Not Really Respect Your Transgender Loved Ones

A common problem for trans people is having loved oneswho want to be supportive, but exhibit unsupportive behaviors. If a loved one in your life recently came out as trans, you likely (to no fault of your own) are exhibiting some of these behaviors.



Extensive Guides

Our Trans Loved Ones

Extensive PFLAG resource to help parents, families, and friends of people who are transgender and gender expansive.

Guide to Being a Trans Ally

Incredibly detailed document from PFLAG explaining everything from basic terms and concepts to how to be a great ally.

ContraPoints

A transgender woman and ex-philosopher YouTuber who takes a unique approach to a variety of issues, including a number of transgender and gender related concepts. Her work can be a lot at times and is not for everyone (she has content warnings for a reason). However, her unique approach often changes the mind of those who normally would not even listen. You do not have to agree with everything she says to get value out of her videos.

Some of the videos I recommend watching are: What is Gender, Gender Dysphoria, Pronouns, and Gender Critical.



Trans Support

PFLAG

PFLAG has many resources: support, information, tools, etc. There are PFLAG chapters all over the United States where transgender or gender-nonconforming individuals and their families can get support.

Shinigami Eyes

A browser addon that highlights transphobic and trans-friendly social network pages and users with different colors. Protect yourself and others from harmful places and people.

Pronoun Dressing room

A site where you can test out new pronouns and names.



Get Involved

Advance the rights and protections of transgender individuals at work, groups, and at home.
