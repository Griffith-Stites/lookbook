mypronouns.org has a number of resources on personal pronouns. This includes why they are important, how to use them, how to handle mistakes, and guides on sharing your own pronouns.

Explains what nonbinary is, gender as a spectrum, nonbinary gender identities, pronouns, and gender-neutral terms.



Allyship

Tips for Allies of Transgender People

Addresses common misconceptions and mistakes made by transgender allies. It addresses backhanded compliments, inappropriate questions, transitions, and how to make your spaces truly trans-inclusive.



Parent Resources

4 Ways Parents Can Process Their Feelings After Their Kid Comes Out As Transgender

Explains the best way to deal with your feelings (as a parent) when your child comes out as trans. It is important to know how to deal with your feelings so they do not get directed to your child.

6 Signs That You Might Not Really Respect Your Transgender Loved Ones

A common problem for trans people is having loved oneswho want to be supportive, but exhibit unsupportive behaviors. If a loved one in your life recently came out as trans, you likely (to no fault of your own) are exhibiting some of these behaviors.

Life Outside the Binary

A nonbinary guide for parents: what that means, and how to begin understanding and supporting your child.

Trans Support

The leading national organization providing crisis intervention and suicide prevention services to lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer & questioning (LGBTQ) young people under 25.

PFLAG

PFLAG has many resources: support, information, tools, etc.There are PFLAG chapters all over the United States where transgenderor gender-nonconforming individuals and their families can get support.

A browser addon that highlights transphobic and trans-friendly social network pages and users with different colors. Protect yourself and othersfrom harmful places and people.
